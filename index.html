Chord Progression

Shuffle a real, commonly-used progression in any key. Every chord is voiced inside one ~4-fret window (a CAGED position anchored on the I chord), so your hand stays put and notes ring into each other.

Roman numerals show each chord’s role in the key (lowercase = minor, a 7/ø suffix sets that chord’s quality). Position 1–5 walks the I chord through the CAGED cycle (E → D → C → A → G shapes) up the neck; every other chord is voiced with whichever of its own shapes lands in the same fret window, so common tones literally stay under your fingers. Standard gives plain triads; Alternate swaps in color tones (sus2, sus4, add9, maj7, maj9, m7, m11, no-3rd) chosen per position for maximum shared notes, with thinner top-string grips. On piano, the position picks the inversion and register.
